Cylinder Force calculation

How to calculate pneumatic cylinder force?

Oct 06

When compressed air is supplied to cylinder ports, it exerts some force on the piston and wall of cylinder. The wall is fixed but piston is free to move. That force exerted on piston causes it to move.

To calculate cylinder extension force

We can calculate this force by using the cylinder piston and piston rod diamater and pressure available. To calculate we need to multiply pressure and area of piston. F = P * A

For example:

        F(kg) = P(bar) * A(cm²)

        pressure (p) = 6bar

        piston diameter (d) = 40mm

        F = p * a

        A = (π * d²)/4

        A = (π * 4²)/4

        A = 12.56cm²

        F = 6 * 12.56

        F = 75.39Kg = 753.9N

753.9N of force is exerted on the cylinder piston. This means 75.39Kg of load can be lifted.(Here we have not considered efficiency. If we consider efficiency by 0.9 then 67Kg can be lifted.)

The Force of cylinder to extend is greater than force of retraction. This is because while extending large area is available but during retraction that area gets subtracted by piston rod area as only that area is affected for backward movement of cylinder.

To calculate retraction force

To calculate retraction force we need to subtract piston rod diameter from piston diameter.

        F(kg) = P(bar) * A(cm²)

        pressure (p) = 6bar

        piston diameter (D) = 40mm

        piston rod diameter (d) = 40mm

        F = p * a

        A = (π * (D²-d²)/4

        A = (π * 4²-2²)/2

        A = 9.42cm²

        F = 6 * 9.42

        F = 56.54Kg = 565.9N

565.9N of force is available for retraction
