Gears Terminologies and formulae

What are different parts of gears and their formulae?

Oct 24

Gear has many important component which play crucial role in selecting gears. Important terms like pitch circle diameter, module, clearance, gear ratios, addenddum, deddendum, numbers of teeth, etc. We will see all of them and their formulae one by one.

Pitch Circle

      Imaginary circle in which rolling of gears takes place in called pitch circle. It has a point called pitch point, at this point two gears are in contact. Due to this rotational motion of driving gears is transfered to driven gear.

Pitch circle diameter is equal to module times numbers of gears.

        P = m * N

        P = Pitch

        m = module

        N = Numbers of gears

Gear Module

      The ratio of pitch circle diameter(in mm) and numbers of teeth is known as module. It is also equal to addendum.

      The ratio of pitch between two consecutive gear teeth is also known as Gear module

        1)m = pd / N

        2)m = P / π

        P = Pitch

        pd = pitch circle diameter

        N = Numbers of gears

Gear Clearance

      When two gears are assembled, then there is a gap between first gear teeth top and another gear teeth bottom. That gap is called clerance.

        c = .167 * m

        m = module

        c = clearance

Addendum

      The Height of gear tooth from pitch circle diameter to the top of teeth is called Addendum. Addendum is equal to module. The Addendum circle is imaginary circle drawn a the top of gearnooth. That circle diameter is known as addendum diameter.

        1)A = m

        2)Ad = pd + 2m

        A = Addendum

        Ad = Addendum diameter

        pd = pitch circle diameter

        m = module

The formula for addendum diameter Ad = pd + 2m consists of pitch circle diameter and 2 times module because we need to consider the gear module in opposite side of gear teeth. As the opposite gear will also have a addendum.

Deddendum

      Deddenum is height of gear teeth from pitch circle diameter to bottom of gear. The imaginary circle at the bottom of the gear is called Deddendum or root diameter.

        Dd = pd - (2m +c)

        A = Addendum

        Ad = Addendum diameter

Numbers of teeth

    Numbers of teeth on external gears is calculated by

        1)N = pd / m

        2)N = (do - 2m) / m

        N = Numbers of teeth on gears

        pd = pitch circle diameter

        do = outer / addendum diameter

        m = module

Teeth height

    Teeth height is sum of addenddum and deddendum,

        h = a + d

        h = teeth height

        a = addendum

        d = deddendum

Gear Ratio

    Gear Ratio for Rotational frequency and numbers of gears is calculated by

        N2/N1 = n1 / n2

        N = Numbers of teeth

        n = rotational frequency

    This means rotational frequency times numbers of teeth of one gear is equal to rotational frequency times numbers of teeth of another gear. This can be represented by the formula

         n1 * N1 = n2 * N2 here n1 is rotational frequency and N is numbers of teeth on the gears.

    Total gear ration can be denoted by the formula given below.

         i = (N2/N1) * (N4/N3) * (N6/N5)...... here i is total gear ratio and N is numbers of teeth on the gears.

Gear Ratio with Pitch circle diameter

    Gear Ratio for Rotational frequency and pitch circle diameter of gears is calculated by

        d2/d1 = n1 / n2

        d = pitch circle diameter

        n = rotational frequency

    This means rotational frequency times pitch circle diameter of one gear is equal to rotational frequency times pitch circle diameter of another gear. This can be represented by the formula

         n1 * d1 = n2 * d2 here n1 is rotational frequency and N is numbers of teeth on the gears.

    Total gear ration can be denoted by the formula given below.

         i = (d2/d1) * (d4/d3) * (d6/d5)...... here i is total gear ratio and d is pitch circle diameter on the gears.
