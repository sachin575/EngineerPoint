How to calculate Tolerance?

Tolerance itself means scope of deviation in dimension. It sepecifies what can be the maximun and minimum deviation for a given dimension. Tolerance has a upper deviation limit and lower deviation limit. With that we can specify a upper dimension i.e what can be the maximum dimension and lower dimension i.e. what can be the minimum dimension. For example. 25±0.2mm. Here we have upper deviation of 0.2mm and lower deviation of 0.2mm. So we can now calculate 25+0.2=25.2mm is the upperlimit of the dimesion gievn. Now 25-0.2=24.8mm is the lower limit. Now we can calculate Tolerance. It is Upper limit - Lower limit. i.e. 25.2 - 24.8=0.4mm is the tolerance. There are standard tables specified by ISO standard for tolerance classes. Now that you have understood the tolerance calculation, with the help of calculator given below find out the tolerance you want.

In ISO standard DIN ISO 286-2 (1990-11),here some tolerance class defined with every dimension. eg 20 H6, here 20 is nominal dimension and H6 is tolerance class. The maximum and minimum deviation for H6 is +0.013 and minimum is 0. It means you have to maintain the workpiece between 20.000 to 20.013.

How to calculate Fits?

Fits define the connection between assembled parts. There are three different types of fits .i.e clearance, transition and interference. Clearance fit means the movement between the consecutive surface will be free. And with no friction. Next is transition fit defines that the movement will be press fit type. That is it will allow some friction. Next is insterference fit, it says that there will be no movement between surfaces. It specifies that once the asssembly is done it will be very rigid. Fits are usually between holes and shaft. To calculate fits we must know the tolerance calculation. There are several tolerance classes. We denote them by capital letters (for holes) and small letters(for shafts). For example30H8/f7.

Q1) Find tolerance for 30 H8/f7

Nominal dimension = 30

Maximum dimension = 30 + 0.033 = 30.033

Minimum dimension = 30 + 0 = 30

For Shaft

Nominal dimension = 30

Maximum dimension = 30 + (-0.020) = 29.980

Max clearance = Max dimension of hole – min dimension of shaft = 30.033 – 29.959 = 0.074

Min clearance = Min dimension of hole – max dimension of shaft = 30.000 – 29.980 = 0.02

Here maximum clearance and minimum clearance both are positive hence its a clearance fit.

  • If maximum clearance is positive and minimum clearance is negative then the fit is transition.
  • If maximum clearance and minimum clearance are negative then the fit is interference.

    • To calculate module(m) of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. m=d/z
    2. p/3.14
    3. da/(2+z)
    4. hf-c
    • d = pitch diameter
    • z = number of teeth
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)
    • da = outer diameter
    • hf = dedendum
    • c = clearance

    To calculate pitch circle diameter of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. m*z
    2. df + 2(m+c)
    3. (p/3.14)*z
    • m = module
    • z = number of teeth
    • df = root diameter
    • c = clearance
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)

    To calculate outside diameter(tip circle dia) of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. d+2.m
    2. m.z+(2.m)
    3. m.(2+z)
    • d = pitch diameter
    • m = module
    • z = number of teeth

    To calculate root circle diameter of gear

    1. d-2(m+c)
    • d = pitch diameter
    • c = clearance
    • m = module

    To calculate pitch of gear teeth

    1. m.3.14
    2. (d.3.14)/z
    3. e+s
    • m = module
    • d = pitch diameter
    • z = number of teeth
    • e = tooth thickness
    • s = space between two gears

    To calculate number of teeth of gear

    1. d/m
    2. (da -2.m)/m
    3. (d*3.14)/p
    • d = pitch diameter
    • m = module
    • da = outer diameter
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)

    To calculate addeddum & dedendum of teeth of gear

    Addendum

    1. addedndum = module
    Dedendum
    1. m+c
    • m = module
    • c = clearance

    To calculate total depth of teeth of gear

    1. (2.m)+c
    2. ha+hf
    • ha = Addendum
    • hf = dedendum

    Screw

    Basic screw are segregated as Head screw, stud bolts and threaded pins

    Head screw are most frequenty used and they have profile on the top. Some are hexagonal solid surface while some have hexagonal slot. Some have minus, plus, star profile on their surface.

    Stud bolts are threaded at their end from both sides.

    Threded pins have threadthrough out their body and have slot at one surface.

    Nuts

    Basic nuts are segregated as hexagonal nuts, cap nut, castle nut, wing nut and grooved nut

    Hexagonal nuts are used when the connection is through out bolted.

    Cap nut are used to close the thread. It protects the thread from corrosion and damage.

    Castle nutare used if the joint is to be secured against any splint or vibration.

    Wing nutare used when connection are unscrewed by hand frequently.

    Information on screw

    We often see screws head marked with as 2.2 or 8.8 or 5.6. They all have significant meaning.

    For example 5.6 Here, 5 defines minimum tensile strength. It is calculated as 5 * 100 = 500N/mm² and 6 defines tensile yeild point and it is calculated as 5 * 6 * 10 = 300N/mm².

    Information on nut For example 8 on nut define, minimum tensile strength. It is calculated as 8 * 100 = 800N/mm²

    Screw locking

    Screw are locked with washer, spring washers, adhesive for avoiding any lossening due to vibration.
