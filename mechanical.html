    • Up Milling
  • Cutter rotates against the direction of feed
  • Also known as conventional milling
  • Chip width is minimum at initial stage and increases with feed. It is maximum at the end of feed.
  • Heat is diffuse to the work piece which causes change in material properties
  • Tool wear is more because the tool runs against the feed.
  • Tool life is short.
  • Less surface finish
  • High quality cutting fluid required
  • High cutting force needed
    • Down Milling
  • Cutter rotates with the direction of feed
  • Also known as climb milling
  • Chip size is maximum at start of the cut And decreases with the feed. It's minimum at the end of feed.
  • No channge in material properties
  • Wear is less as compared as compare to up milling as to cutter rotates with the feed.
  • Tool life is high
  • More finishing than up milling
  • Normal quality cutting fluid will do.
  • Low cutting force is required.

    • Turning is the process in which workpiece is clamped in a rotating chuck and the cutting tools are clamped and moved against it.

    Turning Process
  • Drilling

    Drill is clamped in a chuck, installed in tail stock, and the workpiece is clamped in chuck installed in headstock. The drill is fixed and workpiece rotates. The drill chuck is brought near and the chuck is feeded eith the handle available at the tailstock.

  • Facing Turning

    The cutter is clamped in a certain angle and feeded against the flat surface of the workpiece. This process is called Facing.

  • Plain Turning

    The cutter is clamped at right angle against the rotating axis of the workpiece. The cutter is given the feed depth and moved in paralled to axis. This is plain turning

    • Theory

    To calculate module(m) of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. m=d/z
    2. p/3.14
    3. da/(2+z)
    4. hf-c
    • d = pitch diameter
    • z = number of teeth
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)
    • da = outer diameter
    • hf = dedendum
    • c = clearance

    To calculate pitch circle diameter of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. m*z
    2. df + 2(m+c)
    3. (p/3.14)*z
    • m = module
    • z = number of teeth
    • df = root diameter
    • c = clearance
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)

    To calculate outside diameter(tip circle dia) of gear

    Main Formulas for external gear (spur gear)

    1. d+2.m
    2. m.z+(2.m)
    3. m.(2+z)
    • d = pitch diameter
    • m = module
    • z = number of teeth

    To calculate root circle diameter of gear

    1. d-2(m+c)
    • d = pitch diameter
    • c = clearance
    • m = module

    To calculate pitch of gear teeth

    1. m.3.14
    2. (d.3.14)/z
    3. e+s
    • m = module
    • d = pitch diameter
    • z = number of teeth
    • e = tooth thickness
    • s = space between two gears

    To calculate number of teeth of gear

    1. d/m
    2. (da -2.m)/m
    3. (d*3.14)/p
    • d = pitch diameter
    • m = module
    • da = outer diameter
    • p = pitch(length between centre of two consecutive gears)

    To calculate addeddum & dedendum of teeth of gear

    Addendum

    1. addedndum = module
    Dedendum
    1. m+c
    • m = module
    • c = clearance

    To calculate total depth of teeth of gear

    1. (2.m)+c
    2. ha+hf
    • ha = Addendum
    • hf = dedendum

    Screw

    Basic screw are segregated as Head screw, stud bolts and threaded pins

    Head screw are most frequenty used and they have profile on the top. Some are hexagonal solid surface while some have hexagonal slot. Some have minus, plus, star profile on their surface.

    Stud bolts are threaded at their end from both sides.

    Threded pins have threadthrough out their body and have slot at one surface.

    Nuts

    Basic nuts are segregated as hexagonal nuts, cap nut, castle nut, wing nut and grooved nut

    Hexagonal nuts are used when the connection is through out bolted.

    Cap nut are used to close the thread. It protects the thread from corrosion and damage.

    Castle nutare used if the joint is to be secured against any splint or vibration.

    Wing nutare used when connection are unscrewed by hand frequently.

    Information on screw

    We often see screws head marked with as 2.2 or 8.8 or 5.6. They all have significant meaning.

    For example 5.6 Here, 5 defines minimum tensile strength. It is calculated as 5 * 100 = 500N/mm² and 6 defines tensile yeild point and it is calculated as 5 * 6 * 10 = 300N/mm².

    Information on nut For example 8 on nut define, minimum tensile strength. It is calculated as 8 * 100 = 800N/mm²

    Screw locking

    Screw are locked with washer, spring washers, adhesive for avoiding any lossening due to vibration.
